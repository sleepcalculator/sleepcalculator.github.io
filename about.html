Waking up should feel like a natural beginning.

At Sleep Calculator, we believe that the quality of your day is determined by the precision of your night. Most people wake up feeling groggy not because they didn't sleep long enough, but because they woke up at the wrong time.

The 90-Minute Rule

Human sleep isn't a long, uniform block of rest. Instead, our brains move through distinct sleep cycles. A typical cycle lasts approximately 90 minutes and consists of light sleep, deep sleep, and REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep.

Waking up in the middle of deep sleep causes sleep inertia—that heavy, disoriented feeling that can haunt you for hours. Our mission is to help you time your alarm so you wake up at the end of a cycle, feeling as though you’ve just naturally drifted into consciousness.
